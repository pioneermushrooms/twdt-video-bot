TWDT Season 32: Week 7 Recap
Welcome back to the TWDT Season 32 Week 7 breakdown. The regular season is in the books, the brackets are locked, and if you were betting the spread on Cloud this week, I hope you have a good bankruptcy lawyer. Let’s look at who cashed tickets and who busted bankrolls.

🔥 ON FIRE
Starting with the guys who made the bookies sweat: okyo from Trespass. He’s the first player in Season 32 to sweep both the JAV and WB awards in the same week. He posted a +14 K_net in JAV to dismantle LTG and Revenge, then dropped a +13 in WB for the MVP against Terrorist. Seven weeks of data, and this is the most complete individual performance we’ve seen all tournament.

Then there’s TURBAN from Subnautica. Going 6-0 across all six games isn't just a good week; it’s a robbery. Two JAV MVPs, a WB MVP, and a season-high 182 rating in BASE to take Top Terrier. He closes the regular season as the undisputed most valuable player across all three lanes.

We also need to talk about cripple, another Subnautica heavy hitter, who locked down his sixth consecutive weekly Top Spider award. Nobody else is even in the same zip code. With ratings of 172 and 163, the debate for the best BASE player of the season is dead and buried.

Over on Revenge, Jack played like a guy who knew his contract was up, grabbing a WB MVP and leading a BASE upset over Cloud. The Terrorist squad’s BASE engine also paid out nicely: ro anchored the defense with back-to-back stellar spider ratings, while groan provided the consistency to make that 2-0 happen. Meanwhile, JOESES proved he was worth every penny of his 9.5-star trade value, earning Top Shark honors with a massive 3.1 RPD against Revenge.

Finally, a nod to Spawnisen of LowTierGods. With the pressure cranked up against Terrorist, he dropped his best JAV game of the season—16 kills, 6 deaths, and an MVP to seal a crucial victory.

🧊 ICE COLD
Now for the guys who cost you money. 

It was a disastrous finish for Cloud’s Mythril. At 10 stars for WB and 9 stars for JAV, you expect a premium return. Instead, Mythril went 0-2 in both lanes, dropping deeply negative stats. Cloud needed their ace to step up, and he folded like a cheap suit.

Revenge’s JAV lane completely collapsed, and you can pin the receipts directly on skatarius and Commodo. Both highly billed players went 0-2 with consistently negative numbers. Commodo, in particular, has been the single largest liability for Revenge’s JAV struggles all season.

Over on Terrorist, while their JAV lane clinched a playoff spot, Dak was an absolute anchor in WB. Going 0-2 with negative numbers in both appearances, Dak was the weak link that cost his team a clean sweep.

Cloud’s BASE struggles were perfectly encapsulated by absurd_terr, who posted a dismal 52 rating in a crucial loss to Revenge. Reign’s X-Demo didn’t look much better, dropping 9 deaths against Treyway and following it up with an uninspired 51 rating against Terrorist.

🏆 ROLE AWARDS
Let’s recognize the guys who actually earned their paychecks this week:
•	Top WB & Top JAV: okyo (Trespass) – A clean, historic double-award week.
•	Top Spider: cripple (Subnautica) – A record-breaking sixth consecutive award.
•	Top Terrier: TURBAN (Subnautica) – A season-high rating to cap off a perfect week.
•	Top Shark: JOESES (Terrorist) – The ultimate specialist when it mattered most.

📋 TEAM RECAPS
Let’s look at the final regular season tally.

Subnautica (6-0): Absolute perfection. Sweeping JAV, WB, and BASE, they finish with 32 wins, claiming the #1 seed by right. If you’re betting against them, you hate money.

Trespass (5-1): Dominant across JAV and WB. With 29 wins, they head into the playoffs as the most lethal combat team in the league.

Cloud (0-6): A complete and total collapse. They lost all six games this week, with their star players vanishing entirely. Early-season banking kept them in 4th place, but this was a disastrous finish.

Reign (3-4): Solid enough to be a playoff threat, finishing 3rd with 23 wins. But a structural 0-2 weakness in WB is a massive red flag heading into the postseason.

LowTierGods (3-3): A consistent finish. They dominated WB but faltered in BASE. Still, their early season work secured the 5th seed with 22 wins.

Terrorist (3-3): They did exactly what they needed. A massive JAV win over Treyway sealed their playoff spot, finishing 6th with 18 wins.

Treyway (2-4): Heartbreak. They lost the ultimate playoff-decider to Terrorist and finished 7th with 15 wins. Officially eliminated.

Revenge (3-3): A late WB resurgence and a shocking BASE upset over Cloud showed what this team could have been. They finish dead last with 8 wins, missing the playoffs entirely.

The regular season is done. The stage is set, the brackets are locked, and the playoffs are going to be a bloodbath. Place your bets, and we’ll see you in the postseason.
